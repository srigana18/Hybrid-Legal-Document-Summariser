A Bill to amend the State Education Code to improve digital learning accessibility and support for public school students.  Section 1. The Department of Education shall develop a statewide Digital Learning Access Program focused on providing: (a) Laptops or tablets to students lacking adequate technology at home, (b) High-speed internet subsidies for low-income households, (c) Training programs for teachers on digital instruction methods.  Section 2. School districts must conduct annual technology needs assessments and submit results to the Department.  Section 3. The Department shall allocate grants based on demonstrated district needs, prioritizing rural and underserved communities.  Section 4. Beginning in 2026, the Department shall publish an annual Digital Learning Equity Report evaluating: (a) Technology access rates, (b) Student academic performance trends in digital coursework, (c) Barriers faced by low-income families.  Section 5. Funding for this program shall be provided through a combination of federal education grants and state budget appropriations.  Section 6. This Act shall take effect January 1, 2026.
